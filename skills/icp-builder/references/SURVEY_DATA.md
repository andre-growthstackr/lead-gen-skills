# HubSpot ICP Survey Data
*Source: HubSpot Original Research — "Your Ideal Customer Profile Report" (300+ marketers surveyed)*

Load this file during the conversation to inject relevant stats as light validation moments.
The goal is to surface HubSpot's unique research IP at exactly the right moment —
not as a data dump, but as a single well-timed insight that makes the user feel like
they're getting something they couldn't get from a generic AI prompt.

**Rule:** One stat per moment, anchored to what the user just said. Never list multiple
stats in sequence. Surface them conversationally, not as a bullet list.

**When multiple triggers co-occur in the same turn** (e.g. user has no interviews AND
thin behavioral data AND is early stage), pick the single most relevant stat for what
the user just said and save the others for later turns. Priority order if forced to choose:
1. Behavioral/emotional blind spots stat (Step 5) — highest impact on ICP quality
2. Interview gap stat (Step 3) — most actionable next step
3. Documentation gap stat (Step 1–2) — useful framing but lowest urgency

---

## STEP 1–2 — When the user hasn't built a formal ICP yet, or is starting from scratch

> "53% of marketers don't have a formally documented ICP — so you're in good company
> starting here. The interesting thing: among those who do have one, 93% say it accurately
> represents their most valuable customers. Documentation is the gap, not the knowledge."

Use this to normalize starting the process and set expectation that a documented ICP
has high confidence payoff.

---

## STEP 2 — When asking about business stage

Relevant context (use naturally, not as a stat dump):
- 50% of businesses build their ICP during early growth (33% early growth stage, 17% pre-launch)
- 13% build during maturity stage
- 7% build during a rebrand or refresh
- 3% build after a major product launch

**When to surface:** If the user mentions they're at an early stage, you can affirm:
> "You're in the majority — half of companies build their ICP exactly where you are,
> during early growth. The ones who do it here set themselves up much better than the
> ones who wait until they're scaling."

If they're later stage (maturity/rebrand):
> "Fewer companies do this at your stage — only about 13% build or revisit their ICP
> during maturity. That actually gives you an advantage: you have real retention and
> LTV data to anchor it on, not just early assumptions."

---

## STEP 3 — When flagging the customer interview gap

> "55% of marketers who build strong ICPs rely on direct customer research as their
> primary input — interviews, reviews, feedback. It's the #1 method reported. Even
> a handful of in-depth conversations will surface things no amount of CRM data can."

Use this when the user has no interview data and you're recommending they do some.

---

## STEP 3 — When the user says they're relying on just one data source

> "Most high-performing brands combine several inputs — rarely does one source give
> the full picture. What other perspectives do you have access to beyond [what they mentioned]?"

No specific stat needed here — this is a natural lead-in to broadening the data inputs.

---

## STEP 4 — When the user skips or dismisses cross-functional input

> "Only 19% of marketers involve cross-departmental stakeholders in ICP development —
> but the ones that do consistently produce stronger profiles. Even a quick read from
> your sales team on who closes fastest can change what ends up in the final document."

Use this to make the case for cross-functional input without lecturing.

---

## STEP 5 — When the profile is heavy on demographics/firmographics but thin on behavioral/emotional layers

> "Here's a telling stat: 65% of marketers have solid demographic data on their ICP,
> but only 20% actually understand their customers' pain points — and just 24% know
> where they consume content. Those last two are exactly the layers that predict fit
> and retention most accurately."

This is the most important benchmark in the dataset. Surface it when the user has
good firmographics but hasn't answered the behavioral/emotional probes yet.

---

## STEP 5 — When the user has thin data overall and you're flagging documentation as a gap

> "Only 31% of marketers create formal ICP documentation as part of the process —
> most keep it in their heads or in scattered notes. That's exactly why we're building
> this document: so it can actually be used across sales, marketing, and CS."

Use this to reinforce why the document output matters.

---

## STEP 7 — When running the validation checklist

> "61% of high-performing brands validate their ICP through regular customer surveys,
> 56% track engagement metrics, and 36% rely on sales team feedback. The common thread:
> they treat the ICP as a living document, not a one-time exercise."

Surface this when recommending a quarterly review cadence.

---

## STEP 7 — When the user says their ICP feels too broad or hard to act on

> "The most common ICP challenge marketers report is balancing specificity with market
> opportunity — the fear of being too narrow. But going too broad puts you at risk of
> resonating with no one. An ICP doesn't mean you only serve one type of customer —
> it means you prioritize one in your positioning and GTM."

Use this to validate their concern while reinforcing the case for a tight ICP.

---

## GENERAL — When the user seems uncertain about whether this process is worth it

> "Among marketers who do have a documented ICP, 93% say it accurately represents
> their most valuable customers. The ones who've done this work feel confident in it.
> The gap is almost always in doing it rigorously, not in the concept itself."

Use this as a motivating close when energy is low or the user is questioning the effort.
